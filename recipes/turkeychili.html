Turkey Chili Recipe

Turkey Chili

By Pierre Franey, New York Times

This turkey chili recipe is fabulous, healthy, and super easy to make. Perfect for a cozy fall evening.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 pounds ground turkey, white and dark meat
  • 2 cups coarsely chopped onions
  • 2 tablespoons chopped garlic
  • 1 large sweet red pepper, cored, deveined, and coarsly chopped
  • 1 cup chopped celery
  • 1 jalapeno, cored, deveined and coarsely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh oregano, chopped, or 1 tablespoon dried
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 3 tablespoons chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 3 cups canned diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups chicken broth, fresh or canned
  • salt and black pepper
  • 2 (15-ounce) cancs red kidney beans, drained
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar
  • 1 cup sour cream (optional)
  • sliced lime, for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat the oil over high in a large heavy pot and add the turkey meat. Cook until lightly bronwed, about 5 minutes, chopping down and stirring with the side of a heavy kitchen spoon to break up lumps.
  2. Add the onions, garlic, sweet pepper, celery, jalapeno, oregano, bay leaves, chili powder and cumin. Stir to blend well. Cook for 5 minutes.
  3. Add the tomatoes, chicken broth, salt and pepper to taste. Bring to a boil, reduce heat and simmer, stirring ocassionally, for 15 minutes.
  4. Add the drained beans and cook, stirring ocassionally, for 10 minutes longer. Serve in bowls with cheddar, sour cream, and lime wedges, if desired.
